Updating taboola’s rejection reasons - 

Here’s Why
When I started my content review role at Taboola, a key problem we faced was a high volume of friction with our advertisers. Our team was using outdated and vague rejection reasons like "your landing page is misleading" or "your banner has a policy violation." This was frustrating for our advertisers who were confused about what they needed to fix, and it was a source of constant frustration for our internal account managers who didn't have clear guidance. This was a critical business issue because it created unnecessary work, increased support tickets, and put a strain on our relationships with clients. We were excited about this project because we knew that solving this would not only enhance the user experience but also positively impact the company's bottom line by making our processes more efficient.

Here’s What
The main obstacle we had to overcome was a system of non-specific rejection reasons that lacked the detail needed to empower our advertisers. We needed to transform generic feedback into clear, actionable guidance. For example, instead of a vague "landing page has a policy violation," we needed to tell them exactly what the issue was, such as "there's an intellectual property rights violation” on the landing page, or the product that they’re advertising “is noncompliant in the targeted regions.” The challenge was to take a complex and detailed policy, break it down into specific violation types, and create a comprehensive system of rejection reasons that were both simple to understand and directly linked to the policy.

Here’s How
I took the initiative to analyze our entire policy and identify the most common and confusing violation types. From there, I collaborated with our legal and content teams to create clear and concise rejection reasons. We shifted from a general approach to specific, actionable feedback. For example, a "misleading" landing page now specified the violation, such as "landing page makes unrealistic money making claims” or "medical claims are not substantiated." I also updated our Help Center with detailed descriptions of each new reason, and trained all our regional teams on the new system.
The results were significant. Following the launch of the new rejection reasons, we saw a significant drop in the number of support tickets from advertisers and a noticeable decrease in the number of appeals from account managers. The project was so well-received that I was recognized with a company award. This project not only enhanced the overall user experience but also streamlined our internal operations and created a more efficient review process for everyone involved.
